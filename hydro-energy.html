HYDROPOWER ENERGY

Hydropower, or hydroelectric power, uses the energy of flowing or falling water to generate electricity. Dams are often built on rivers to store water in reservoirs; when released, the water flows through turbines, generating electricity. Hydropower is one of the oldest and most widely used renewable energy sources.

Why is Hydropower Important?

Hydropower is a reliable and flexible source of electricity. It provides a stable power supply and can be quickly adjusted to meet peak demand. Hydropower plants also offer benefits such as flood control, irrigation, and water supply.

What is the Role of Hydropower in Clean Energy Transitions?

Hydropower contributes significantly to global renewable electricity generation. It supports grid stability and integration of other intermittent renewable sources like wind and solar. Small-scale hydropower projects are particularly valuable in remote areas without access to the grid. Hydroelectricity generation increased by almost 70 TWh (up close to 2%) in 2022, reaching 4,300 TWh. Hydropower remains the largest renewable source of electricity, generating more than all other renewable technologies combined.

Advantages

  • Reliable and provides continuous power
  • Can store energy through pumped storage

Challenges

  • Can disrupt local ecosystems
  • Requires large infrastructure investment

Use Cases

  • Large-scale hydroelectric dams
  • Small river-based hydro projects
