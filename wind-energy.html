WIND ENERGY

Wind energy captures the kinetic energy of moving air using wind turbines. The wind turns the blades of the turbine, which spins a shaft connected to a generator, producing electricity. Wind energy is clean and sustainable, and modern wind farms can generate significant amounts of electricity.

Why is Wind Energy Important?

Wind energy is one of the fastest-growing renewable energy technologies. It can be deployed onshore and offshore, allowing for flexible siting options. Wind farms provide substantial economic benefits to local communities through job creation and land lease payments.

What is the Role of Wind Energy in Clean Energy Transitions?

Wind energy plays a critical role in reducing reliance on fossil fuels and cutting greenhouse gas emissions. It is one of the predominant sources of power generation in the Net Zero Emissions by 2050 Scenario. As technology advances, the efficiency and capacity of wind turbines continue to improve, making wind energy increasingly cost-competitive with traditional energy sources. In 2022, wind electricity generation increased by a record 265 TWh (up 14%), reaching more than 2,100 TWh. This was the second highest growth among all renewable power technologies, behind solar PV.

Advantages

  • No carbon emissions
  • Highly scalable (small turbines to large wind farms)

Challenges

  • Needs consistent wind
  • Can impact bird migration and local environments

Use Cases

  • Offshore and onshore wind farms
  • Small wind turbines for residential power
